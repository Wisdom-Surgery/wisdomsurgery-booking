Pre-operative Instructions

Please read carefully before your surgery. Contact us if you have any questions.

Fasting Requirements

If having General Anaesthesia or IV Sedation:

  • Nothing to eat or drink for 6 hours before your procedure — this includes water, chewing gum, and lollies.
  • Clear fluids are permitted up to 2 hours before — clear water, black tea or coffee (no milk), or clear apple juice (no pulp or milk-based drinks).
  • If you take regular morning medications, discuss with your surgeon or anaesthetist whether to take these with a small sip of water on the morning of surgery.
⚠ Important: Failure to fast correctly may result in your surgery being cancelled on the day for your safety. If you are unsure about any food or drink, do not consume it and contact us.

If having Local Anaesthetic only:

No fasting is required. You may eat and drink normally before your appointment. A light meal is recommended.

Medications
  • Continue taking most regular medications unless specifically instructed otherwise by your surgeon.
  • STOP blood thinners (aspirin, warfarin, clopidogrel, rivaroxaban, apixaban) if instructed — your surgeon will provide specific guidance on timing. Do not stop these without instruction.
  • Bring a current list of all medications (including dose and frequency) to your appointment.
  • Do not take herbal supplements or fish oil for 7 days before surgery — these can affect bleeding and wound healing.
  • If you are diabetic, discuss insulin and oral medication adjustment with your GP before surgery to manage blood glucose safely on fasting day.
On the Day of Surgery
  • Arrive at the time instructed — allow extra time for paperwork and pre-operative preparation. Do not be late.
  • Bring with you: Medicare card, private health insurance card, photo ID, any X-rays or scans, and your current medication list.
  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing with sleeves that can easily roll up above the elbow (for IV access).
  • Remove nail polish, jewellery, piercings, and contact lenses before arriving.
  • Do not wear makeup or perfume.
  • Brush your teeth before arriving at the clinic or hospital.
Transport & Supervision
If having General Anaesthesia or IV Sedation — a responsible adult escort is mandatory:
  • You must have a responsible adult to drive you home. You cannot use a taxi, rideshare, or public transport unaccompanied.
  • You cannot drive, operate machinery, or make important legal or financial decisions for 24 hours after sedation or general anaesthesia.
  • A responsible adult should stay with you for the first night following surgery.

If a responsible escort cannot be arranged, please contact us before your surgery date — we may need to reschedule.

If You Are Unwell Before Surgery

If you develop a fever, cold, flu, chest infection, or any other significant illness in the week before your surgery, contact us immediately. Surgery may need to be postponed for your safety and the safety of other patients.

If you are pregnant or suspect you may be pregnant, advise your surgeon urgently before the procedure. Some medications and techniques used in surgery are not safe during pregnancy.
