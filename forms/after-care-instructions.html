Home Care After Oral Surgery

Patient

Date

  1. Bleeding: Light traces of blood are normal in the first 24 hours. For active bleeding, avoid exertion and raised blood pressure. Bite on gauze for 10–20 minutes. Ongoing bleeding? Contact us.
  2. Pain Relief: Alternate paracetamol and ibuprofen every 3 hours. If pain is greater than 5/10, use Endone or Palexia as prescribed. Most stop prescription relief after 3 days; simple pain relief may be needed for 7 days. Avoid ibuprofen on an empty stomach.
  3. Antibiotics: Take as prescribed:
  4. Swelling: Significant swelling is expected, peaking on day 3, subsiding over 3–4 days. Around-the-clock ice packs for 3 days help prevent swelling. Eat cold foods, keep head elevated.
  5. Nausea: If ongoing nausea occurs and anti-nausea medication is needed, contact your surgeon.
  6. Oral Hygiene: Do not brush or rinse the first 24 hours. After that, use warm salt water. Savacol (chlorhexidine), or Difflam (numbing) 3 times daily for up to 2 weeks — max 1 month to avoid staining.
  7. Food: Soft foods. Limited mouth opening is normal; it takes about 2 weeks before eating larger or chewy foods.
  8. Rest: Rest for a few days; avoid strenuous activity.
  9. Sutures: Sutures loosen by day 5–7 and dissolve by day 10–14. It's normal for sockets to slightly open and food to collect — keep them clean with gentle mouthwash and brush other teeth as normal.
  10. Smoking & Vaping: Smoking or vaping greatly increases the risk of dry socket and infection. Do not smoke or vape for at least 48 hours after surgery.
  11. Follow-Up: We will call you one business day after surgery to check progress, provide documentation, and arrange a follow-up (face-to-face or telehealth in several weeks). We'll advise if scans are needed.
  12. Return to Work/School: Usually after 7 days. We provide medical/carer certificates and discuss this during follow-up.
  13. Emergency: Contact your surgeon within 24 hours if pain or swelling are still increasing after day 4, sockets emit pus or foul odour, or nausea or vomiting persists with dehydration concerns.
