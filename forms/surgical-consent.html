Risks & Complications

No surgical procedure is without risk. Your surgeon has recommended this procedure because the expected benefits outweigh the risks. Please read the following carefully.

General surgical risks — common to all surgical procedures:

Pain and discomfort — expected after any surgery, managed with prescribed or over-the-counter pain relief.
Swelling and bruising — typically peaks at 48–72 hours and resolves over 1–2 weeks.
Bleeding — some bleeding is expected; persistent or heavy bleeding may require further management.
Infection — may require antibiotics or further treatment; signs include increasing pain, swelling, fever, or discharge after day 3.
Scarring — all incisions leave some degree of scarring; placement is planned to minimise visibility.
Anaesthetic reactions — uncommon; your anaesthetist will assess your individual risk.
Nerve sensitivity — temporary altered sensation in the area of surgery; usually resolves without treatment.
Need for further surgery — a small number of patients require an additional procedure to address complications or achieve the desired result.
Unexpected intraoperative findings — the surgeon may need to modify the planned procedure if findings during surgery require it, always in your best interest.
